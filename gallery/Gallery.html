Do you Know?

Women always love receiving gifts, no matter what the occasion is. Whether it's a birthday, anniversary, or just a surprise present, it always brings a smile to their face. But why do women love gifts so much?

Firstly, it shows that someone has taken the time and effort to think about them and what they might like. It's a way of showing appreciation and affection towards them. It also makes them feel special and valued.

Secondly, receiving gifts can be a form of self-care for women. It's a way of treating themselves and indulging in something they might not have bought for themselves otherwise. It can also be a way of relieving stress and improving their mood.

History of International Women's Day

The history of International Women's Day dates back to the early 20th century when women around the world were fighting for equal rights and freedom. On March 8th, 1908, more than 15,000 women in the United States marched in New York, demanding shorter working hours, higher wages, and equality with men. This was a significant event in the campaign for women's suffrage.

Then, in 1910, at the International Women's Conference in Copenhagen, Denmark, March 8th was chosen as the International Women's Day to honor women's achievements and raise awareness of women's rights. Since then, this day has become an occasion to celebrate and show respect for women worldwide.

International Women's Day has become an important day to highlight issues related to women, including gender equality, employment, education, health, and family life. It is also an opportunity to honor the achievements of women in various fields and express gratitude to the women who have made a difference in our lives.

Since 1977, the United Nations has officially recognized International Women's Day as an international holiday. Each year, International Women's Day is celebrated with activities, events, and campaigns to honor and promote women's rights worldwide.
